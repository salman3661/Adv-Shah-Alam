  - h2: "What Is a False Criminal Case?"
    content: "<p>A false criminal case is one where a person is accused of a crime they did not commit. In Bangladesh, this often takes the form of a fabricated First Information Report (FIR) filed at a police station, or a private complaint (Naraji Petition) filed directly in a Magistrate Court.</p>\n<p>False cases are sadly common in Bangladesh's legal landscape — used as instruments of harassment, business rivalry, land disputes, family feuds, or political pressure. The accused has the law firmly on their side, but only if they act quickly and strategically.</p>"
  - h2: "Common Reasons False Cases Are Filed"
    content: "<p>Understanding why a false case has been filed helps your lawyer choose the right legal strategy. Common reasons include:</p>\n<ul>\n<li><strong>Land and property disputes:</strong> A neighbour or relative files a criminal case to pressure you into giving up land or property rights</li>\n<li><strong>Business rivalry:</strong> Competitors fabricate allegations of cheque dishonour, fraud, or corruption</li>\n<li><strong>Family disputes:</strong> Divorce, inheritance, or domestic matters become criminal allegations to harass the other party</li>\n<li><strong>Political or personal vendetta:</strong> Enemies use connections to lodge police complaints without genuine basis</li>\n<li><strong>Extortion:</strong> A criminal case is filed with the expectation of out-of-court payment to withdraw it</li>\n</ul>\n<p>In all these situations, early legal action is the single most important factor in a successful defence.</p>"
  - h2: "Your Legal Rights When Falsely Accused"
    content: "<p>Bangladesh's Constitution and the Code of Criminal Procedure (CrPC) 1898 protect individuals from wrongful prosecution. Key rights include:</p>\n<ul>\n<li><strong>Right to bail:</strong> Both bailable and non-bailable offences have bail mechanisms — your lawyer can seek bail from the Magistrate, Sessions Court, or High Court</li>\n<li><strong>Right to legal representation:</strong> You are entitled to a lawyer at every stage of proceedings</li>\n<li><strong>Right to challenge the FIR:</strong> Courts have the power to quash (cancel) an FIR if it is found to be frivolous, malicious, or an abuse of process</li>\n<li><strong>Right to file a counter case:</strong> You can file a case against the complainant for malicious prosecution and defamation</li>\n<li><strong>Right to a fair trial:</strong> The prosecution must prove guilt beyond reasonable doubt — you are presumed innocent</li>\n</ul>\n<p>These rights are enforceable — but passivity is dangerous. A false case left unchallenged can result in arrest, remand, and even conviction if evidence is fabricated.</p>"

    content: "<p>If you fear imminent arrest on a false case, your lawyer can file for <strong>anticipatory bail</strong> in the Sessions Court or the High Court Division under Section 498 of the CrPC. This grants you temporary protection from arrest while the case is being heard.</p>\n<p>Anticipatory bail is particularly critical when:</p>\n<ul>\n<li>The FIR involves a non-bailable offence (murder, robbery, serious assault)</li>\n<li>There is reason to believe police may arrest without investigation</li>\n<li>The complaint appears politically or personally motivated</li>\n</ul>\n<p>The court will consider factors including whether your presence is necessary for investigation, whether you are a flight risk, and whether the allegations appear prima facie genuine.   - h2: "Step 3 – Challenge the FIR in Court"
    content: "<p>Once your lawyer reviews the FIR, they may advise challenging it before the Magistrate or Sessions Court. The grounds for challenging an FIR include:</p>\n<ul>\n<li>The alleged act does not constitute a criminal offence as described</li>\n<li>The FIR was filed with malicious intent and lacks bona fide basis</li>\n<li>Material facts in the FIR are demonstrably false</li>\n<li>The case is time-barred</li>\n</ul>\n<p>Your lawyer may present alibi evidence, character witnesses, documentary proof, or CCTV/digital evidence to demonstrate the false nature of the complaint at the earliest stage possible.</p>"
  - h2: "Step 4 – File a Writ Petition in the High Court"
    content: "<p>One of the most powerful remedies against a false criminal case in Bangladesh is a <strong>Writ Petition</strong> filed in the High Court Division of the Supreme Court of Bangladesh under Article 102 of the Constitution.</p>\n<p>Through a writ, the High Court can:</p>\n<ul>\n<li><strong>Quash the FIR entirely</strong> — permanently cancelling the criminal case</li>\n<li><strong>Stay the trial</strong> — preventing the case from proceeding while the High Court reviews it</li>\n<li><strong>Issue a Rule</strong> — directing the police or prosecution to justify why the case should not be dismissed</li>\n</ul>\n<p>Quashing a false FIR via writ petition is the most definitive remedy. It requires demonstrating that the complaint is an abuse of process — which is very achievable when the FIR is clearly fabricated. A <a href=\"/services/supreme-court-lawyer\" style=\"color:var(--accent)\">Supreme Court lawyer in Bangladesh</a> with High Court experience is essential for this step.</p>"

faqs:
  - question: "Can I get an FIR cancelled in Bangladesh?"
    answer: "Yes. The High Court Division can quash an FIR through a writ petition if it finds the case to be frivolous, malicious, or an abuse of process. Your lawyer can also request the Magistrate to drop the case if investigation fails to support the charges."
  - question: "What should I do immediately after learning of a false FIR against me?"
    answer: "Contact a criminal lawyer immediately. Do not speak to police without your lawyer present. Do not attempt to contact the complainant directly. Your lawyer will assess the FIR, secure bail if needed, and map out a legal strategy."
  - question: "Can I sue the person who filed a false case against me?"
    answer: "Yes — after the case is resolved in your favour, you can file a malicious prosecution civil suit for damages. You can also file a criminal defamation case under the Penal Code if their false allegations damaged your reputation."
  - question: "What is the difference between quashing an FIR and getting acquitted?"
    answer: "Quashing an FIR (through a writ) eliminates the case entirely before trial. Acquittal happens at the end of a trial where you are found not guilty. Quashing is far faster and more definitive — it is the preferred outcome in false case situations."
  - question: "Are false criminal cases common in Bangladesh?"
    answer: "Unfortunately, yes. False cases are frequently filed in land disputes, family conflicts, business rivalries, and political matters. Bangladesh courts are increasingly alert to this — a skilled lawyer can identify and expose these patterns effectively."
  - question: "Can I get anticipatory bail even for serious non-bailable offences?"
    answer: "Yes. The Sessions Court and High Court Division have power to grant anticipatory bail even in non-bailable cases, though the court weighs gravity of the offence, likelihood of flight, and whether the application is bona fide. An experienced bail lawyer substantially improves success prospects."
