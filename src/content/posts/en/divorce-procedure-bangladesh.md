  - h2: "Legal Framework for Divorce in Bangladesh"
    content: "<p>Divorce in Bangladesh is governed by personal law depending on the religion of the parties. For Muslims — the majority — the <strong>Muslim Family Laws Ordinance 1961 (MFLO)</strong> and the <strong>Muslim Marriages and Divorces (Registration) Act 1974</strong> are the primary laws. For Hindus, the dissolution of marriage is still highly restricted under traditional law, while for Christians the <strong>Divorce Act 1869</strong> applies.</p>\n<p>All divorce proceedings ultimately involve the <strong>Family Court</strong> established under the Family Courts Ordinance 1985, which has exclusive jurisdiction over matrimonial disputes in Bangladesh, including divorce, maintenance, dower (mahr), and child custody.</p>"
  - h2: "Types of Divorce for Muslims"
    content: "<p>Muslim law in Bangladesh recognises three main forms of divorce:</p>\n<ul>\n<li><strong>Talaq:</strong> Husband-initiated divorce by pronouncement, subject to MFLO notice requirements.</li>\n<li><strong>Khula:</strong> Wife-initiated divorce where the wife surrenders her dower (mahr) or other consideration in exchange for release from the marriage.</li>\n<li><strong>Mubarat:</strong> Divorce by mutual agreement of both parties.</li>\n<li><strong>Judicial Divorce (Faskh):</strong> Granted by the Family Court on grounds such as cruelty, desertion, or failure to maintain.</li>\n</ul>\n<p>Each type has a different procedure. Consulting a <a href=\"/divorce-lawyer-uttara\" style=\"color:var(--accent)\">divorce lawyer in Uttara</a> ensures you choose and follow the correct route.</p>"
  - h2: "Talaq: How It Works"
    content: "<p>Under the MFLO 1961, a husband wishing to pronounce talaq must follow a strict statutory procedure:</p>\n<ol>\n<li>Pronounce talaq (verbally or in writing) — this does not immediately dissolve the marriage.</li>\n<li>Within 30 days of pronouncement, give <strong>written notice to the Chairman of the Union Parishad/Pourashava</strong> of the area where the wife lives.</li>\n<li>The Chairman constitutes an Arbitration Council to attempt reconciliation within 90 days.</li>\n<li>If reconciliation fails after the 90-day waiting period (iddat), the divorce becomes effective.</li>\n</ol>\n<p>Failure to notify the Chairman is a criminal offence under the MFLO. Triple talaq in one sitting is not recognised as three separate divorces — it counts as a single revocable talaq under Bangladesh law.</p>"
  - h2: "Khula: Wife-Initiated Divorce"
    content: "<p>Khula is the right of a Muslim wife to seek dissolution of marriage by returning her mahr (dower) or other agreed compensation. In Bangladesh, a wife can pursue khula through two routes:</p>\n<ul>\n<li><strong>Mutual Agreement:</strong> If the husband consents, both parties give notice to the Chairman, follow the 90-day reconciliation period, and the divorce is registered.</li>\n<li><strong>Through Family Court:</strong> If the husband refuses, the wife files a suit in Family Court for judicial khula. The court has the power to decree the dissolution and determine the appropriate compensation.</li>\n</ul>\n<p>Women have an absolute right to seek khula. The court cannot compel a wife to remain married against her will.</p>"
  - h2: "Judicial Divorce Through Family Court"
    content: "<p>Either spouse may file a divorce suit in the Family Court on the following grounds:</p>\n<ul>\n<li>Cruelty (physical or mental)</li>\n<li>Desertion for two or more years</li>\n<li>Failure to maintain for two or more years</li>\n<li>Imprisonment of the other spouse for seven or more years</li>\n<li>Communicable disease (e.g., leprosy)</li>\n<li>Impotency</li>\n<li>Insanity for two or more years</li>\n</ul>\n<p>Filing a suit requires submitting a plaint (application) to the Family Court, paying court fees, and attending hearings. Proceedings typically take six months to two years depending on the court's backlog and whether the opposing party contests.</p>"
  - h2: "Divorce for Non-Muslims"
    content: "<p>Hindu personal law in Bangladesh does not have a codified divorce statute, making divorce extremely difficult for Hindus. A Hindu spouse may file a civil suit for judicial separation but a full dissolution of marriage remains legally complex. Conversion to another religion dissolves the marriage under certain interpretations.</p>\n<p>Christians in Bangladesh may seek divorce under the <strong>Divorce Act 1869</strong> on grounds of adultery (with other additional grounds for wives such as cruelty or desertion). The petition is filed in the High Court or a District Court with jurisdiction.</p>"
  - h2: "Required Documents for Divorce"

    answer: "A verbal talaq is the starting point, but it is not effective until the 90-day MFLO notice and arbitration process is completed."
  - question: "What is mahr and must a husband pay it?"
    answer: "Mahr (dower) is a financial right of the wife specified in the Kabinnama. It is payable on demand or upon divorce, unless the wife forgoes it in a khula divorce."
  - question: "Can a divorced wife claim maintenance in Bangladesh?"
    answer: "A wife is entitled to iddat maintenance and outstanding mahr. Long-term maintenance may be claimed in appropriate cases through the Family Court."
  - question: "What happens to children after divorce?"

  - question: "Does divorce need to be registered?"
