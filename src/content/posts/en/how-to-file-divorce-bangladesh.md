  - h2: "Step 1: Gather Your Documents"
    content: "<p>Before taking any legal action, collect these documents:</p>\n<ul>\n<li>✅ Original Kabinnama (nikah certificate / marriage registration document)</li>\n<li>✅ National Identity Cards (NID) of both husband and wife</li>\n<li>✅ Birth certificates of children (if applicable)</li>\n<li>✅ Any prior agreements, court orders, or property documents</li>\n<li>✅ Proof of address (utility bill, rent agreement)</li>\n</ul>\n<p><strong>Most critical:</strong> The Kabinnama. Without it, the process cannot begin. If lost, obtain a certified copy from the Nikah Registrar (Kazi) who conducted your marriage.</p>"
  - h2: "Step 2: Husband Filing Divorce (Talaq) — Exact Process"
    content: "<p>If you are the husband and wish to file for divorce:</p>\n<ol>\n<li><strong>Pronounce talaq</strong> — verbally or in writing.</li>\n<li><strong>Draft a formal written Notice</strong> addressed to the Chairman of your local Union Parishad (UP) or Ward Commissioner (City Corporation). This notice must state: your name, wife's name, Kabinnama details, and the pronouncement of talaq.</li>\n<li><strong>Send the notice by registered post</strong> to the Chairman AND simultaneously deliver a copy to your wife. Keep the postal receipt.</li>\n<li>The Chairman constitutes an <strong>Arbitration Council</strong> and begins reconciliation efforts within 30 days.</li>\n<li>Wait the mandatory <strong>90-day period</strong> from the Chairman's receipt of the notice.</li>\n<li>If no reconciliation, the talaq is effective on Day 90. Collect your <strong>Divorce Certificate from the UP</strong>.</li>\n</ol>\n<p><strong>⚠️ Do NOT:</strong> Pronounce triple talaq and consider it done. Under Bangladesh law (MFLO 1961), all talaq must follow this notification process. Skipping it is a criminal offence.</p>"
  - h2: "Step 3: Wife Filing Divorce (Khula or Judicial) — Exact Process"
    content: "<p><strong>Option A: Khula (if husband agrees)</strong></p>\n<ol>\n<li>Negotiate terms with your husband — typically returning dower (mahr).</li>\n<li>Draft a written khula notice with agreed terms.</li>\n<li>Send the notice to the UP Chairman; wait 90 days.</li>\n<li>Collect divorce certificate after Day 90.</li>\n</ol>\n<p><strong>Option B: Judicial Divorce (if husband refuses)</strong></p>\n<ol>\n<li>Consult a <a href=\"/divorce-lawyer-uttara\" style=\"color:var(--accent)\">family lawyer</a> to assess which legal grounds apply to your case.</li>\n<li>Your lawyer drafts a <strong>Plaint</strong> (petition) for the Family Court.</li>\n<li>File the plaint at the Family Court in your or your husband's district.</li>\n<li>The court issues summons to your husband.</li>\n<li>Attend scheduled hearings; your lawyer presents evidence and arguments.</li>\n<li>Court issues a decree dissolving the marriage.</li>\n</ol>"
  - h2: "Step 4: Filing at the Correct Court"
    content: "<p>Which court you approach depends on what stage and type of legal action you need:</p>\n<ul>\n<li><strong>Union Parishad / City Corporation:</strong> For the mandatory notification and arbitration process for talaq, khula, and mubarat.</li>\n<li><strong>Family Court (Judge Court):</strong> For judicial divorce petitions, custody applications, maintenance claims.</li>\n<li><strong>Sessions Court / High Court Division:</strong> For bail applications, appeals against Family Court decisions.</li>\n</ul>\n<p>In Dhaka Metropolitan area (including Uttara), the relevant Family Court is located at the Dhaka District Judgeship. Your lawyer will know the correct courtroom and filing procedure.</p>"
  - h2: "Step 5: The 90-Day Period — What You Must Do"
    content: "<p>The 90-day waiting period is mandatory under Section 7 of the MFLO 1961. It cannot be waived even if both parties agree. During this period:</p>\n<ul>\n<li>The Union Parishad Chairman will attempt reconciliation</li>\n<li>The divorce is NOT yet legally effective — you are still married during this time</li>\n<li>If the wife is pregnant, the divorce effect postpones until after delivery</li>\n<li>The husband cannot marry another woman during this period</li>\n</ul>\n<p><strong>Use this time wisely:</strong> Negotiate and document in writing all financial settlements (mahr, maintenance, property division) and child custody arrangements. Get these reviewed by your lawyer before signing anything.</p>"
  - h2: "Step 6: Getting Your Divorce Certificate"
    content: "<p>After the 90-day period expires without reconciliation, go to the Union Parishad / Ward Commissioner's office to collect your official <strong>Divorce Certificate</strong>. This certificate:</p>\n<ul>\n<li>Is the legal proof that you are divorced</li>\n<li>Is required for future marriage registration</li>\n<li>Is needed for NID corrections, passport updates, property transfers</li>\n<li>Should be kept safely — multiple certified copies are advisable</li>\n</ul>\n<p>Do not delay collecting this certificate. There is no automatic expiry but delays create practical complications.</p>"

faqs:
  - question: "Where do I file for divorce in Bangladesh?"
    answer: "For talaq/khula: send notice to your local Union Parishad Chairman or Ward Commissioner (City Corporation). For judicial divorce: file a petition at the Family Court in your district."
  - question: "Can I file for divorce online in Bangladesh?"
    answer: "Currently there is no full online filing system for divorce in Bangladesh. The notice to the Union Parishad must be physically delivered or sent by registered post. Your lawyer can handle all filings on your behalf."
  - question: "What is the first step to get a divorce in Bangladesh?"
    answer: "Gather your Kabinnama (marriage certificate) and both parties' NIDs. Then send a written divorce notice to the local Union Parishad Chairman — this is the legally required first step for Muslim divorce in Bangladesh."
  - question: "Can I file for divorce if my husband is abroad?"
    answer: "Yes. If your Kabinnama includes a delegated right of divorce (talaq-e-tawfiz), you can exercise it. Alternatively, you can file for judicial divorce in Family Court regardless of your husband's location."
  - question: "How long after filing does divorce become final in Bangladesh?"
    answer: "Minimum 90 days after the Chairman receives the notice (for talaq/khula/mubarat). For Family Court judicial divorce, from 6 months to several years depending on the case."
  - question: "What proof do I need to file for divorce in Bangladesh?"
    answer: "The Kabinnama is the primary proof of marriage needed to start divorce proceedings. Additional documents depend on the type of divorce and whether the case involves children or property disputes."
