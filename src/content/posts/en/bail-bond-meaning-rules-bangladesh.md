sections:
  - h2: "What Is a Bail Bond in Bangladesh?"
    content: "<p>A bail bond (also called a bail recognizance) is a formal written undertaking — signed by the accused person and one or more sureties (guarantors) — that is submitted to the court when bail is granted.</p>\n<p>It is a legal promise that:</p>\n<ul>\n<li>The accused will appear before the court on every date they are required to</li>\n<li>The accused will not flee the jurisdiction</li>\n<li>The accused will comply with all specified conditions</li>\n</ul>\n<p>In Bangladesh, bail bonds are governed by <strong>Sections 499–502 of the Code of Criminal Procedure 1898 (CrPC)</strong>. The bond creates personal and financial liability for both the accused and the surety if its conditions are violated.</p>"
  - h2: "Who Must Sign the Bail Bond?"
    content: "<p>Typically, the following must sign the bail bond:</p>\n<ul>\n<li><strong>The accused person</strong> — personal recognizance</li>\n<li><strong>One or more sureties</strong> (guarantors) — unless the court grants bail without surety (personal bond only)</li>\n</ul>\n<p>The court specifies the number of sureties and the amount of the bond. For minor offences, the court may accept a personal bond by the accused alone, without any surety.</p>"
  - h2: "Types of Bail Bonds in Bangladesh"
    content: "<p>There are two main types of bail bonds in Bangladesh:</p>\n<ul>\n<li><strong>Personal Bond (PR Bond):</strong> Only the accused signs. No external surety is required. The accused personally guarantees they will appear in court. This is typically granted for minor offences or when the accused is considered highly trustworthy.</li>\n<li><strong>Surety Bond:</strong> Both the accused and a surety (guarantor) sign. The surety provides property documents as security. This is the most common type for serious or non-bailable offences. The court sets the bond amount — the surety's property must be worth at least that amount.</li>\n</ul>"
  - h2: "What Is a Surety and What Do They Risk?"
    content: "<p>A surety is a person who guarantees to the court that the accused will appear for all hearings. The surety:</p>\n<ul>\n<li>Must be an adult Bangladeshi citizen with NID</li>\n<li>Must own property of sufficient value (as determined by the court)</li>\n<li>Must submit property documents (deed, mutation record, land records) with the bail application</li>\n</ul>\n<p><strong>What does the surety risk?</strong> If the accused fails to appear in court — without an acceptable excuse — the court can issue a show-cause notice to the surety and, if unsatisfied, <em>forfeit</em> the surety bond. This means the surety can be required to pay the full bond amount to the court. Their property is also at risk of legal action.</p>\n<p>Being a surety is a serious legal responsibility. Make sure you understand this before agreeing to act as someone's guarantor.</p>"
  - h2: "How Much Is a Bail Bond?"
    content: "<p>The court sets the bail bond amount at its discretion, based on:</p>\n<ul>\n<li>The severity of the alleged offence</li>\n<li>The perceived risk of the accused fleeing</li>\n<li>The financial capacity of the accused and surety</li>\n</ul>\n<p>In practice, for minor offences in Bangladesh, bail bonds may be set at a few thousand taka (often symbolic). For serious non-bailable offences in the Sessions Court or High Court, bail bonds can range from ৳50,000 to ৳5,00,000 or more.</p>\n<p><strong>Note:</strong> The bond amount is not a payment to the court — it is only forfeited if bond conditions are violated. No cash needs to be deposited unless the court specifically orders a cash bond (very rare).</p>"
  - h2: "Conditions Typically Attached to Bail"
    content: "<p>Along with the bail bond, courts routinely attach conditions such as:</p>\n<ul>\n<li>The accused must appear in court on every scheduled date</li>\n<li>The accused must not leave the district (or country) without court permission</li>\n<li>The accused must surrender their passport</li>\n<li>The accused must report to the local police station periodically</li>\n<li>The accused must not contact or intimidate any witness</li>\n<li>The accused must not tamper with evidence</li>\n</ul>\n<p>Additional conditions may be specific to the case. Violating any condition automatically puts the bail at risk of cancellation.</p>"
  - h2: "What Happens If Bail Bond Conditions Are Violated?"
    content: "<p>Violating bail bond conditions triggers a serious legal process:</p>\n<ol>\n<li>The court issues a <strong>warrant of arrest</strong> for the accused.</li>\n<li>The court issues a <strong>show-cause notice</strong> to the surety — asking why the bond amount should not be forfeited.</li>\n<li>If the surety cannot provide a satisfactory answer, the court issues a <strong>bond forfeiture order</strong> — the surety must pay the full bond amount.</li>\n<li>If the surety cannot pay, their property may be seized and sold to realize the amount.</li>\n<li>The accused, once re-arrested, is unlikely to get bail again easily for the same case.</li>\n</ol>"
  - h2: "How to Find a Surety for Bail in Bangladesh"
    content: "<p>Finding a surety can be one of the most stressful parts of getting bail in Bangladesh. Here's what to know:</p>\n<ul>\n<li>The surety should ideally be a close family member or trusted person with property in the same district</li>\n<li>The surety must be willing — courts do not accept reluctant or involuntary sureties</li>\n<li>The surety's property must be free from other encumbrances (mortgages, disputes)</li>\n<li>In some cases, particularly for women, courts may relax surety requirements</li>\n</ul>\n<p>Your lawyer will advise on what property documentation the specific court requires for a surety at your bail hearing.</p>"
  - h2: "Common Problems with Bail Bonds"
    content: "<ul>\n<li><strong>❌ Surety's property is disputed or mortgaged:</strong> Courts will reject the surety documents. Make sure the property is clean.</li>\n<li><strong>❌ Surety's property is in a different district:</strong> Some courts require the surety to hold property within the same district.</li>\n<li><strong>❌ Incomplete surety documents:</strong> Missing mutation records or land records cause delay.</li>\n<li><strong>❌ Accused missing a court date:</strong> Even one missed hearing without prior permission can trigger bond forfeiture proceedings.</li>\n<li><strong>❌ Not understanding conditions:</strong> Conditions beyond appearing in court (passport surrender, travel restrictions) are often not explained clearly to the accused. Always ask your lawyer to explain all conditions in detail.</li>\n</ul>"

  - question: "How much does a bail bond cost in Bangladesh?"
    answer: "The court sets the bail bond amount. For minor offences, it may be a few thousand taka (symbolic). For serious offences in the Sessions Court or High Court, it can range from ৳50,000 to ৳5,00,000 or more."
  - question: "Do I need to pay cash for bail in Bangladesh?"
    answer: "No. In Bangladesh, bail bonds normally do not require actual cash deposits to the court. The bond amount is only paid (forfeited) if the accused violates bail conditions. The surety provides property documents, not cash."
  - question: "What happens if the surety wants to withdraw from the bail bond?"
    answer: "A surety can apply to the court to be discharged from the bond. The court will require the accused to provide a new surety before releasing the original one. The accused may be remanded to custody until a new surety is found."
  - question: "Can bail bond conditions be changed in Bangladesh?"
    answer: "Yes. Either party can apply to the court to modify bail conditions — for example, to allow the accused to travel abroad for medical treatment. The court has discretion to modify or relax conditions on valid grounds."
  - question: "What is a personal bond (PR bond) in Bangladesh?"
    answer: "A personal recognizance (PR) bond is bail granted on the accused's own promise to appear in court, without any external surety. It is typically granted for minor offences or very trustworthy accused persons."
