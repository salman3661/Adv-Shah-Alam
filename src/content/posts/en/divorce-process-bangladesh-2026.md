  - h2: "Types of Divorce Under Bangladesh Law"
    content: "<p>In Bangladesh, divorce for Muslims is governed by the <strong>Muslim Family Laws Ordinance 1961 (MFLO)</strong>. There are three main types:</p>\n<ul>\n<li><strong>Talaq:</strong> Husband-initiated divorce. The husband pronounces talaq and notifies the Union Parishad Chairman.</li>\n<li><strong>Khula:</strong> Wife-initiated divorce. The wife seeks release, usually by returning part of her mahr (dower).</li>\n<li><strong>Mubarat (Mutual):</strong> Both spouses agree to end the marriage jointly.</li>\n<li><strong>Judicial Divorce:</strong> Filed through Family Court when the husband refuses divorce or specific legal grounds exist.</li>\n</ul>\n<p>For non-Muslims, divorce is governed by separate personal laws (Christian Divorce Act 1869, etc.). This guide focuses on Muslim divorce law. A qualified <a href=\"/divorce-lawyer-uttara\" style=\"color:var(--accent)\">divorce lawyer in Dhaka</a> can assess which route applies to your situation.</p>"
  - h2: "Step-by-Step: Talaq (Husband-Initiated Divorce)"
    content: "<p>This is the most common form of divorce in Bangladesh. Here is exactly what must happen:</p>\n<ol>\n<li>The husband pronounces talaq (verbally, in writing, or otherwise).</li>\n<li>He must <strong>immediately</strong> send a written notice to the Chairman of the local Union Parishad / City Corporation Ward Commissioner <strong>and</strong> a copy to the wife simultaneously.</li>\n<li>The Chairman forms an Arbitration Council within 30 days to attempt reconciliation.</li>\n<li>A mandatory <strong>90-day waiting period</strong> starts from the date the Chairman receives the notice.</li>\n<li>If reconciliation fails, the talaq becomes legally effective on Day 90.</li>\n<li>Collect the <strong>Divorce Certificate</strong> from the Union Parishad after Day 90.</li>\n</ol>\n<p><strong>⚠️ Critical Warning:</strong> Pronouncing talaq without sending written notice to the Chairman is a criminal offence under Section 6 of the MFLO 1961. The divorce is also NOT legally effective until the notice requirement is fulfilled. Many people skip this and face serious problems later.</p>"
  - h2: "Step-by-Step: Khula (Wife-Initiated Divorce)"
    content: "<p>A Khula is the wife's legal right to seek divorce. The process:</p>\n<ol>\n<li>The wife communicates her intention to divorce to her husband.</li>\n<li>Both parties negotiate terms — usually the wife returns her dower (mahr) or part of it.</li>\n<li>Once the husband agrees, the khula notice is sent to the Union Parishad Chairman.</li>\n<li>The same 90-day reconciliation period applies.</li>\n<li>After 90 days, if no reconciliation, the divorce is effective and a certificate is issued.</li>\n</ol>\n<p>If the husband refuses to agree to khula, the wife's option is a <strong>Judicial Divorce</strong> through Family Court. An experienced <a href=\"/divorce-lawyer-uttara\" style=\"color:var(--accent)\">family lawyer in Uttara</a> can help petition the court on specific legal grounds.</p>"
  - h2: "Step-by-Step: Judicial Divorce via Family Court"
    content: "<p>A wife can file for judicial divorce at the Family Court when the husband refuses. Valid legal grounds include:</p>\n<ul>\n<li>Husband's failure to pay maintenance for 2+ years</li>\n<li>Husband sentenced to 7+ years imprisonment</li>\n<li>Husband's cruelty (physical or mental)</li>\n<li>Husband's insanity for 2+ years</li>\n<li>Husband's impotence at time of marriage</li>\n<li>Husband taking a second wife without permission (MFLO violation)</li>\n</ul>\n<p>The process: File a <em>plaint</em> at the Family Court → Serve summons on the husband → Attend hearings → Court delivers decree. Cases typically take 6 months to 3 years depending on complexity.</p>"
  - h2: "Required Documents"
    content: "<p>Gather these documents before starting the divorce process:</p>\n<ul>\n<li>Original <strong>Kabinnama</strong> (marriage registration certificate) — the most critical document</li>\n<li>National Identity Cards (NID) of both parties</li>\n<li>Written talaq notice (for husband-initiated divorce)</li>\n<li>Proof of delivery of notice to the Union Parishad Chairman</li>\n<li>Birth certificates of any children</li>\n<li>For Family Court cases: plaint, supporting affidavit, proof of legal grounds</li>\n<li>Any prior court orders or property agreements</li>\n</ul>\n<p><strong>Note:</strong> If you cannot find your Kabinnama, you can obtain a certified copy from the Nikah Registrar (Kazi) who performed your marriage. Do not start the process without this.</p>"

This cannot be shortened.</li>\n<li><strong>Judicial Divorce (uncontested):</strong> Approximately 6–12 months.</li>\n<li><strong>Judicial Divorce (contested):</strong> 1–5 years depending on the court's schedule and complexity.</li>\n</ul>\n<p>The 90-day period is non-negotiable.   - h2: "Common Mistakes to Avoid"
    content: "<p>These errors cause real-world legal problems for thousands of Bangladeshis every year:</p>\n<ul>\n<li><strong>❌ Pronouncing talaq without written notice to the Chairman:</strong> This is an offence and the divorce is not legally effective.</li>\n<li><strong>❌ Not getting a Divorce Certificate:</strong> Without the official certificate, you cannot prove you are legally divorced.</li>\n<li><strong>❌ Remarrying before Day 90:</strong> A marriage within the 90-day period is void and illegal.</li>\n<li><strong>❌ Ignoring financial rights:</strong> Not claiming outstanding mahr, maintenance, or dowry goods.</li>\n<li><strong>❌ Verbal agreements on custody:</strong> Children's custody arrangements must be formalized through court or a legally binding written agreement.</li>\n<li><strong>❌ Assuming Triple Talaq is effective:</strong> Under Bangladesh law, triple talaq in one sitting is treated as a single revocable talaq — not an immediate divorce.</li>\n</ul>"
  - h2: "Financial Rights After Divorce"
    content: "<p>Divorce in Bangladesh does not erase financial obligations. Key rights include:</p>\n<ul>\n<li><strong>Mahr (Dower):</strong> The full unpaid mahr becomes payable immediately upon divorce.</li>\n<li><strong>Iddat Maintenance:</strong> The husband must maintain the wife for the iddat period (~90 days) after divorce takes effect.</li>\n<li><strong>Child Maintenance:</strong> The father must support all children regardless of custody arrangement.</li>\n<li><strong>Dowry Return:</strong> All jewelry and gifts given to the wife at marriage must be returned.</li>\n</ul>\n<p>These rights are enforceable by the Family Court. Never accept verbal promises on these matters — always get written documentation.</p>"

    answer: "Original Kabinnama (marriage certificate), NIDs of both parties, written talaq notice, proof of notice delivered to Chairman, birth certificates of children (if any), and for court cases, a plaint and affidavit."

  - question: "Can a wife file for divorce in Bangladesh without the husband's consent?"
    answer: "Yes. A wife can file for Judicial Divorce in Family Court on specific legal grounds (cruelty, non-maintenance, imprisonment etc.) even if the husband refuses to consent."
  - question: "Is triple talaq valid in Bangladesh?"
    answer: "No. Under Bangladesh law and the MFLO 1961, triple talaq in one sitting is treated as a single revocable talaq. Proper written notice to the Chairman is mandatory regardless of the number of talaq pronouncements."
  - question: "What happens if I don't send notice to the Union Parishad Chairman?"
    answer: "Failure to send notice is a criminal offence under Section 6 of the MFLO 1961 (punishable by fine or imprisonment). More importantly, the divorce does not become legally effective until the notice procedure is properly completed."
  - question: "How do I prove I am divorced in Bangladesh?"
    answer: "The official Divorce Certificate issued by the Union Parishad Chairman after the 90-day period is the accepted legal proof of divorce. Always collect and safely store this document."
