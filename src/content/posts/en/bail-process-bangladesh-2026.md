  - h2: "What Is Bail and Why Does It Matter?"
    content: "<p>Bail is the legal mechanism that allows an arrested person to be released from custody while their case is being heard — on the condition they will appear in court when required. It is governed by <strong>Sections 496–502 of the Code of Criminal Procedure 1898 (CrPC)</strong>.</p>\n<p>The fundamental legal principle: an accused is innocent until proven guilty. Keeping someone in custody before conviction can devastate families, destroy livelihoods, and create irreversible harm — even if the person is ultimately acquitted.</p>\n<p>Getting bail quickly requires knowing exactly which court has jurisdiction, what documents to file, and how to argue your case effectively. A skilled <a href=\"/bail-lawyer-dhaka\" style=\"color:var(--accent)\">bail lawyer in Dhaka</a> can make all the difference.</p>"
  - h2: "Types of Bail in Bangladesh"
    content: "<p>Bangladesh law recognises these distinct types of bail:</p>\n<ul>\n<li><strong>Regular Bail (Section 497 CrPC):</strong> Applied after arrest, at the Magistrate Court or Sessions Court depending on the stage and severity of the offence.</li>\n<li><strong>Anticipatory Bail:</strong> Applied <em>before</em> arrest when a person has reason to believe they may be arrested. Available from the Sessions Court or the High Court Division.</li>\n<li><strong>Interim Bail:</strong> Temporary bail granted on an urgent basis while the main bail application is pending.</li>\n<li><strong>High Court Bail:</strong> Applied at the High Court Division of the Bangladesh Supreme Court when lower courts have refused bail or for serious offences.</li>\n<li><strong>Bail on Bond:</strong> Release secured by a personal surety bond — a guarantor pledges to ensure the accused appears in court.</li>\n</ul>"
  - h2: "Who Can Apply for Bail?"
    content: "<p>The following can apply for bail:</p>\n<ul>\n<li>The accused person (even without a lawyer, though not recommended)</li>\n<li>A lawyer on behalf of the accused</li>\n<li>A close family member through a lawyer</li>\n</ul>\n<p><strong>For bailable offences</strong> (listed in Schedule 1 of CrPC): Bail is a <em>right</em> — it must be granted if applied for. Police or the court cannot refuse it.</p>\n<p><strong>For non-bailable offences</strong> (serious crimes: murder, robbery, rape etc.): Bail is <em>discretionary</em> — the court decides based on the facts of the case. This is where you absolutely need an experienced criminal lawyer.</p>"
  - h2: "Step-by-Step: How to Apply for Bail in Bangladesh"
    content: "<p>Follow these exact steps when someone has been arrested:</p>\n<ol>\n<li><strong>Contact a lawyer immediately</strong> — ideally a criminal lawyer with experience in the relevant court. Time is critical after arrest.</li>\n<li>Your lawyer obtains the <strong>FIR copy, GD copy</strong> and case details from the police station.</li>\n<li>Your lawyer <strong>drafts a bail petition</strong> citing: the case number, the charges, the accused's background, and legal grounds for release (no flight risk, no evidence tampering risk, family responsibilities, health etc.).</li>\n<li>The petition is filed at the <strong>appropriate court</strong> — Magistrate Court for most cases; Sessions Court for serious offences or if already charge-sheeted; High Court for very serious cases or when lower courts refuse.</li>\n<li>The court schedules a <strong>bail hearing</strong>, typically the same day or the next working day.</li>\n<li>Both the defence lawyer and the public prosecutor argue; the judge decides.</li>\n<li>If granted, the accused signs the bail bond with a surety and is released.</li>\n</ol>\n<p>In urgent situations, your lawyer can apply for <strong>interim bail</strong> to get the accused released within hours while the formal application is processed.</p>"
  - h2: "Required Documents for Bail Application"
    content: "<p>Prepare these documents before going to court:</p>\n<ul>\n<li>Copy of the FIR (First Information Report) or GD (General Diary)</li>\n<li>Copy of the charge sheet (if filed)</li>\n<li>National Identity Card (NID) of the accused</li>\n<li>NID of the surety (guarantor)</li>\n<li>Land/property documents of the surety (to prove they can stand as guarantor)</li>\n<li>Medical certificates (if health grounds are cited in the application)</li>\n<li>Employment or business documents (to prove ties to the community)</li>\n<li>Any character references (from local leaders, employers etc.)</li>\n</ul>"
  - h2: "Bail Hearing: What the Judge Considers"
    content: "<p>During the bail hearing, the judge weighs these factors:</p>\n<ul>\n<li><strong>Nature and gravity of the alleged offence</strong> — murder or robbery applications face much higher scrutiny than minor offences</li>\n<li><strong>Prior criminal record</strong> of the accused</li>\n<li><strong>Flight risk</strong> — will the accused flee the country or hide?</li>\n<li><strong>Evidence tampering risk</strong> — will the accused tamper with witnesses or destroy evidence?</li>\n<li><strong>Community ties</strong> — family, employment, local roots that make flight unlikely</li>\n<li><strong>Health and age</strong> — elderly, ill, or very young accused may receive favourable consideration</li>\n<li><strong>Duration of custody</strong> — long pre-trial detention without charge weakens the prosecution's stance</li>\n</ul>"
  - h2: "What to Do If Bail Is Refused"
    content: "<p>If the Magistrate Court refuses bail:</p>\n<ol>\n<li>Appeal immediately to the <strong>Sessions Court</strong> (District Judge).</li>\n<li>If the Sessions Court also refuses, apply to the <strong>High Court Division</strong> of the Bangladesh Supreme Court.</li>\n<li>For cases involving serious human rights violations or arbitrary detention, a <strong>writ of habeas corpus</strong> can be filed before the High Court Division.</li>\n</ol>\n<p>Each level of appeal requires a fresh, stronger bail petition. An experienced <a href=\"/supreme-court-lawyer-bangladesh\" style=\"color:var(--accent)\">Supreme Court lawyer in Bangladesh</a> handles High Court bail applications for the most serious cases.</p>"

faqs:
  - question: "How long does it take to get bail in Bangladesh?"
    answer: "Magistrate Court bail: 1–7 days. Sessions Court bail: 3–14 days. High Court bail: 7–30+ days. In urgent cases, interim bail can be heard the same day the application is filed."
  - question: "What is the difference between bailable and non-bailable offences in Bangladesh?"
    answer: "For bailable offences (Schedule 1 CrPC), bail is a right and must be granted. For non-bailable offences (murder, robbery, serious crimes), bail is discretionary and the court decides based on case facts."
  - question: "Can bail be cancelled after it is granted in Bangladesh?"
    answer: "Yes. The court can cancel bail if: bail conditions are violated, the accused tampers with evidence or witnesses, or if new evidence emerges showing the accused is a flight risk."
  - question: "What is anticipatory bail in Bangladesh?"
    answer: "Anticipatory bail is applied BEFORE arrest when a person believes they may be arrested. It is available from the Sessions Court or High Court Division and temporarily protects against arrest."
  - question: "Can a person be arrested again while on bail in Bangladesh?"
    answer: "Yes. A new FIR can lead to a new arrest. Violation of bail conditions or a court warrant can also result in re-arrest even while bail is in effect for another case."
  - question: "Is a surety required for all bail applications in Bangladesh?"
    answer: "For most bail applications, yes — a surety (guarantor) who provides their NID and property documents is required. Some cases may allow personal bail without surety for minor offences."
  - question: "What courts handle bail in Bangladesh?"
    answer: "Magistrate Courts and Sessions Courts handle most bail applications. The High Court Division of the Bangladesh Supreme Court handles bail when lower courts refuse or for very serious offences."
