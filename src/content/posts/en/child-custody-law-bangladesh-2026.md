  - h2: "What Is Child Custody Under Bangladesh Law?"
    content: "<p>Child custody law in Bangladesh is primarily governed by two frameworks:</p>\n<ul>\n<li><strong>Muslim Personal Law (Hizanat):</strong> For Muslim families, Islamic principle of Hizanat determines who has the right to physical custody of young children.</li>\n<li><strong>Guardian and Wards Act 1890:</strong> Governs legal guardianship and is used by courts of all religions when appointing a legal guardian for children.</li>\n</ul>\n<p>There is an important distinction: <strong>Physical custody (Hizanat)</strong> — who the child lives with day-to-day; and <strong>Legal guardianship</strong> — who has the legal authority to make decisions about the child's property, education, and well-being. Both may be held by different parties.</p>"
  - h2: "Who Has the Legal Right to Child Custody?"
    content: "<p>Under Muslim law in Bangladesh, the general rule is:</p>\n<ul>\n<li><strong>Mother</strong> has the primary right of physical custody (Hizanat) for young children — sons until age 7, daughters until puberty (approximately 15).</li>\n<li><strong>Father</strong> is the natural legal guardian of the child's person and property.</li>\n<li>After the custody ages above, the father typically assumes physical custody, but courts always consider the child's welfare above rigid age rules.</li>\n</ul>\n<p>The mother <strong>loses</strong> her right to Hizanat if she: remarries (a non-relative), is found unfit (substance abuse, neglect), or voluntarily relinquishes custody. A <a href=\"/divorce-lawyer-uttara\" style=\"color:var(--accent)\">custody lawyer in Dhaka</a> can assess your specific situation.</p>"
  - h2: "Age and Gender Rules in Muslim Custody Law"
    content: "<p>The classical age rules under Hizanat in Bangladesh are:</p>\n<ul>\n<li><strong>Sons:</strong> Mother has custody until age <strong>7</strong>. After 7, custody passes to the father unless the court determines the child's welfare requires otherwise.</li>\n<li><strong>Daughters:</strong> Mother has custody until <strong>puberty</strong> (typically 15). After that, custody may pass to the father.</li>\n</ul>\n<p><strong>Important:</strong> Bangladesh courts increasingly apply a <em>welfare of the child</em> standard that can override these traditional age rules. If a court finds the father's home environment is better for the child's welfare — even for a 5-year-old son — the father may be awarded custody. The child's welfare is always paramount.</p>"
  - h2: "Factors Courts Consider in Custody Decisions"
    content: "<p>When the Family Court decides a custody dispute, it weighs all of these factors:</p>\n<ul>\n<li>The child's age and maturity</li>\n<li>The child's own wishes (if old enough to express a preference)</li>\n<li>Each parent's moral character and fitness</li>\n<li>Each parent's financial capacity to maintain the child</li>\n<li>The child's educational and religious requirements</li>\n<li>Physical and mental health of each parent</li>\n<li>Continuity — which parent has been the primary caregiver</li>\n<li>Proximity to the child's school and support network</li>\n</ul>\n<p>The court will never award custody to a parent who is found to be abusive, habitually intoxicated, or otherwise unfit, regardless of the age-based rules.</p>"
  - h2: "How to Apply for Child Custody in Bangladesh"
    content: "<p>To apply for child custody through the courts:</p>\n<ol>\n<li>Consult an experienced <a href=\"/divorce-lawyer-uttara\" style=\"color:var(--accent)\">family lawyer</a> to assess your case and determine the grounds for custody.</li>\n<li>Your lawyer files a <strong>custody petition</strong> under the Guardian and Wards Act 1890 at the Family Court in your district.</li>\n<li>The court issues notice to the other parent.</li>\n<li>Both parents attend hearings; the court may appoint a welfare officer to interview the child.</li>\n<li>The court issues a custody order based on the child's best interests.</li>\n<li>If custody needs to be transferred, the court enforces the order through a bailiff if necessary.</li>\n</ol>\n<p><strong>Pro tip:</strong> File the custody petition simultaneously with or immediately after filing for divorce. Custody applications filed early demonstrate commitment and prevent the other parent from taking the child abroad or to another location.</p>"

  - h2: "Visitation Rights for the Non-Custodial Parent"
    content: "<p>The parent who does not have physical custody retains the right to visitation (access) with the child. Bangladesh courts typically set out a visitation schedule in the custody order, specifying:</p>\n<ul>\n<li>Regular weekly or bi-weekly visitation days and times</li>\n<li>Holiday and Eid visitation arrangements</li>\n<li>School holiday arrangements</li>\n<li>Conditions under which visitation may be supervised</li>\n</ul>\n<p>Denying visitation without a court order is contempt of court and can result in sanctions against the custodial parent.   - h2: "Can Custody Orders Be Changed?"
    content: "<p>Yes. Custody orders are not permanent. A parent can apply to the Family Court to <strong>modify a custody order</strong> if there has been a material change in circumstances, such as:</p>\n<ul>\n<li>The custodial parent remarries</li>\n<li>The custodial parent is relocating abroad</li>\n<li>Evidence of abuse, neglect, or the parent becoming unfit</li>\n<li>The child's wishes change as they grow older</li>\n<li>Significant change in either parent's financial or living situation</li>\n</ul>\n<p>Courts take modification applications seriously. You will need to demonstrate that the change is in the child's best interests, not merely in your own convenience.</p>"

  - question: "Who gets custody of children in Bangladesh after divorce?"
    answer: "Under Muslim law, the mother typically has physical custody (Hizanat) — sons until age 7, daughters until puberty. After those ages, the father may assume custody. However, courts can override these rules based on the child's best interests."
  - question: "Can a father get custody of children in Bangladesh?"
    answer: "Yes. The father is the natural legal guardian of the child. He can obtain physical custody when children exceed the Hizanat ages, or earlier if the court finds it is in the child's best interests."
  - question: "How do I apply for child custody in Bangladesh?"
    answer: "File a custody petition under the Guardian and Wards Act 1890 at the Family Court in your district. A family lawyer can prepare the petition and represent you throughout the proceedings."
  - question: "Can the mother lose custody in Bangladesh?"
    answer: "Yes. A mother loses her right to Hizanat (physical custody) if she remarries a non-relative, is found unfit (neglect, abuse, substance abuse), or voluntarily relinquishes custody."
  - question: "Can a custody order be changed in Bangladesh?"
    answer: "Yes. Either parent can apply to the Family Court to modify a custody order if there is a material change in circumstances — such as the custodial parent relocating abroad, remarrying, or becoming unfit."
  - question: "What happens if my spouse takes my child abroad without permission?"
    answer: "This is child abduction under Bangladesh law. Contact the police immediately and file a habeas corpus petition before the High Court Division of the Supreme Court for return of the child."
  - question: "Does the child's preference matter in custody decisions in Bangladesh?"
    answer: "Yes. For older children who can express a clear preference, courts give significant weight to the child's wishes. The older and more mature the child, the more influential their preference becomes."
