  - h2: "Legal Framework: The Special Marriage Act 1872"
    content: "<p>The <strong>Special Marriage Act 1872</strong> — a colonial-era law still in force in Bangladesh — provides the legal framework for civil marriages. Key features:</p>\n<ul>\n<li>Marriages under this Act are registered with a Marriage Registrar appointed by the government</li>\n<li>A marriage certificate is issued which is legally equivalent to any other marriage registration certificate</li>\n<li>The Act requires a 30-day advance notice to the Marriage Registrar before the ceremony</li>\n<li>Any person can lodge an objection within 30 days of the notice — the Registrar must investigate before proceeding</li>\n<li>The marriage is solemnized in the presence of three witnesses</li>\n</ul>\n<p>Note: Many people in Bangladesh informally call any officially registered marriage a 'court marriage' — including nikah registration at a Kazi's office. The Special Marriage Act 1872 is specifically for non-religious civil registration.</p>"
  - h2: "Step-by-Step Court Marriage Procedure in Bangladesh"
    content: "<p>The formal court marriage procedure under the Special Marriage Act 1872:</p>\n<ol>\n<li><strong>Give Notice:</strong> Both parties jointly give written notice to the Marriage Registrar in the district where at least one of them has lived for at least 30 days. The notice includes personal details, address, and a declaration of intent to marry.</li>\n<li><strong>30-Day Waiting Period:</strong> The notice is displayed publicly for 30 days. Anyone may object during this period. If no valid objection is lodged, the marriage proceeds.</li>\n<li><strong>Sign the Declaration:</strong> Both parties appear before the Marriage Registrar, sign a declaration in the prescribed form, and three witnesses also sign.</li>\n<li><strong>Marriage Solemnized:</strong> The Marriage Registrar signs and seals the register. The marriage is now formally solemnized.</li>\n<li><strong>Marriage Certificate Issued:</strong> A marriage certificate is issued immediately — this is your legal proof of marriage.</li>\n</ol>\n<p>Many couples engage a lawyer to handle the notice drafting, documentation, and coordination with the Registrar's office to ensure everything is procedurally correct. <a href=\"/divorce-lawyer-uttara\" style=\"color:var(--accent)\">Contact a family lawyer in Dhaka</a> for assistance.</p>"

  - h2: "Is Court Marriage Valid Under Bangladesh Law?"
    content: "<p>Yes. A court marriage performed under the Special Marriage Act 1872 is fully legally valid in Bangladesh. The marriage certificate issued is:</p>\n<ul>\n<li>Recognized by all government agencies in Bangladesh</li>\n<li>Accepted for visa applications, passport, NID updates</li>\n<li>Valid proof of marriage for property and inheritance purposes</li>\n<li>Internationally recognized (though some countries may require apostille / legalization)</li>\n</ul>\n<p>However, court marriage under the Special Marriage Act does NOT give the parties the same personal law rights as a nikah in terms of mahr, talaq, and inheritance — since those are governed by Muslim personal law.   - h2: "Difference Between Court Marriage and Nikah"
    content: "<p>Many people confuse these two terms. Here is the key distinction:</p>\n<ul>\n<li><strong>Nikah</strong> is an Islamic marriage contract performed under Muslim personal law. It is registered by a licensed Kazi (Nikah Registrar) and produces a Kabinnama. Rights like mahr, talaq, and Islamic inheritance apply.</li>\n<li><strong>Court Marriage (Special Marriage Act 1872)</strong> is a purely civil marriage registration. It is secular — no religious ceremony is required. It applies when both parties want civil law to govern their marriage, or for interfaith couples.</li>\n</ul>\n<p>In practice, many Bangladeshis do BOTH — perform a nikah for religious validity AND register under civil law for documentary purposes. A family lawyer can advise which approach best protects your interests.</p>"
